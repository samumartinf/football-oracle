How the Football Oracle works

The Oracle blends three statistical models to predict match outcomes, optimized for the Mon Petit Prono (MPP) scoring system — where rarity and contrarian picks earn bonus points.

Elo ratings (25% weight) — A power-rating system that estimates team strength from historical international results. Higher Elo → higher win probability. Seeded from eloratings.net and updated with live tournament results.

Decomposed Poisson (70% weight) — Models goals scored and conceded using per-team attack/defense strengths derived from goal history. Produces a full score probability matrix with Dixon-Coles adjustment (corrects for the low-scoring correlation in football). When teams lack goal data, weight shifts to Elo automatically.

Market odds (5% weight) — A weak gravitational pull from the betting market. Doesn't make the Oracle a crowd-follower, but prevents extreme contrarian bets when all signals disagree.

Rarity bonus — The MPP game rewards contrarian picks. ≤2% crowd → +200 bonus, ≤5% → +100, ≤10% → +50, ≤15% → +25. The EV calculation includes this bonus.

EV delta threshold — A contrarian pick must beat the crowd-favorite EV by ≥5 points to justify the variance. No marginal bets.

Contrarian cap — Maximum 4 contrarian picks per matchday, enforced by the batch optimizer. Keeps the weakest contrarian bets as safe picks.
